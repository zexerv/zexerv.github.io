Academia & Research

Exploring the intersection of control theory, robotics, and artificial intelligence.

Research Interests

My research spans the intersection of robotics, control systems, and machine learning with a focus on developing intelligent systems that can learn from experience and adapt to complex, dynamic environments.

Robotics & Automation

Human-robot interaction, programming by demonstration, motion planning, and trajectory optimization for robotic manipulators.

Reinforcement Learning

Application of RL algorithms to robotics problems, path planning, decision-making under uncertainty, combining model-based control with learning.

Control Systems

Nonlinear control theory, model predictive control, optimal control strategies, and constrained control problems for complex mechanical systems.

Autonomous Navigation

Navigation algorithms for aerial robots, visual-inertial odometry, sensor fusion, and obstacle avoidance in GPS-denied environments.

Current Research

ARTO: Automated Robotics for Testing Optimization

MERLIN Laboratory, Polytechnic University of Milan

October 2024 - Present

Developing intuitive programming-by-demonstration frameworks for industrial robots, enabling non-technical operators to program complex trajectories through natural demonstration.

Key contributions:

  • Extension of OMPL motion planners in MoveIt for PbD
  • Learning algorithms to interpret human demonstrations (joystick)
  • Specialized cost functions within RRT algorithms
  • Optimization techniques for trajectories with singularity avoidance

Reinforcement Learning for Robot Path Planning

Polytechnic University of Milan

2023

Using reinforcement learning techniques for robot path planning in environments with static obstacles. Combining traditional planning with learning-based methods for robust navigation.

Research highlights:

  • Custom reward functions (efficiency, safety, smoothness)
  • CNN preprocessing for voxelized obstacle data
  • Formulation of manipulability metrics for singularity avoidance
  • Comparative analysis of RL algorithms for continuous action spaces
