Academia & Research

Exploring the intersection of control theory, robotics, and artificial intelligence

Research Interests

My research spans the intersection of robotics, control systems, and machine learning with a focus on developing intelligent systems that can learn from experience and adapt to complex, dynamic environments.

Robotics & Automation

Human-robot interaction, programming by demonstration, motion planning, and trajectory optimization for robotic manipulators. My current work focuses on developing intuitive interfaces for robot programming through demonstration.

Reinforcement Learning

Application of reinforcement learning algorithms to robotics problems, particularly in path planning and decision-making under uncertainty. I'm exploring ways to combine model-based control with learning-based approaches.

Control Systems

Nonlinear control theory, model predictive control, and optimal control strategies for complex mechanical systems. I have particular interest in constrained control problems and their application to robotics.

Autonomous Navigation

Navigation algorithms for aerial robots, with emphasis on visual-inertial odometry, sensor fusion, and obstacle avoidance in GPS-denied environments.

Current Research

ARTO: Automated Robotics for Testing Optimization

MERLIN Laboratory, Polytechnic University of Milan

October 2024 - Present

This research focuses on developing intuitive programming-by-demonstration frameworks for industrial robots. The goal is to enable non-technical operators to efficiently program complex robot trajectories through natural demonstration.

Key contributions include:

  • Extension of OMPL motion planners in MoveIt for programming by demonstration
  • Development of learning algorithms to interpret human demonstrations captured via joystick inputs
  • Implementation of specialized cost functions within RRT algorithms to accurately interpret human intention
  • Optimization techniques for robotic trajectories with singularity avoidance

This work bridges the gap between intuitive human demonstration and precise robotic execution, potentially reducing programming time for complex trajectories by up to 65%.

Reinforcement Learning for Robot Path Planning

Polytechnic University of Milan

2023

This project focuses on using reinforcement learning techniques for robot path planning in environments with static obstacles. The approach combines traditional planning algorithms with learning-based methods to develop robust navigation strategies.

Research highlights:

  • Custom reward functions balancing efficiency, safety, and path smoothness
  • Preprocessing pipeline using CNNs to transform voxelized obstacle data
  • Formulation of manipulability metrics to avoid singularities during continuous movement
  • Comparative analysis of RL algorithms for continuous action spaces in robotic manipulation

Publications

Journal Articles

Saeidi, M., et al.

"Nonlinear Tracking Control Algorithm for Dynamical Output Systems Manipulated by the Hardly Constrained Oscillatory Actuator"

Structural Control and Health Monitoring, 2023

DOI: 10.1155/2023/3799011 Abstract

This paper introduces a novel nonlinear tracking control algorithm for dynamical output systems with constraints on the actuation mechanism. The proposed approach addresses challenges in systems where traditional control methods fail due to actuation limitations. We demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness through both theoretical analysis and experimental validation on an oscillatory mechanical system.

Saeidi, M., et al.

"Synthetic Lyapunov stabilization technique for designing actuation-constrained multi-input multi-output control systems"

Journal of the Franklin Institute, 2022

DOI: 10.1016/j.jfranklin.2021.12.022 Abstract

This research presents a synthetic Lyapunov stabilization technique for designing control systems in multi-input multi-output (MIMO) scenarios where actuation constraints present significant challenges. We develop a novel approach that guarantees system stability while respecting practical limitations on actuator performance. The method is demonstrated through both simulation and experimental validation on a mechanical control system.

Saeidi, M., et al.

"Pattern Recognition of Unbalanced Rigid Rotor Bearing Forces"

Amirkabir Journal of Mechanical Engineering, 54(12), pp. 3591-3606, 2022

Link Abstract

This study presents a novel pattern recognition approach for identifying and characterizing forces in unbalanced rigid rotor bearing systems. We develop and validate advanced computational methods to detect patterns in vibration signatures that indicate specific types of imbalance. The approach provides a robust method for condition monitoring and predictive maintenance in rotating machinery.

Conference Proceedings

Saeidi, M., et al.

"Constrained Control of Moving Base Robotic System with Cooperative Arms"

30th Annual International Conference of the Iranian Association of Mechanical Engineers, Tehran, Iran, 2022

Abstract

This paper addresses the complex control challenges in moving base robotic systems with cooperative manipulator arms. We propose a constrained control framework that maintains stable performance despite the dynamic coupling between the mobile base and the mounted manipulators. Experimental results demonstrate improved performance in tasks requiring coordination between locomotion and manipulation.

Saeidi, M., et al.

"Pattern Recognition of Unbalanced Rigid Rotor Bearing Forces"

18th National Conference and 7th International Conference on Manufacturing Engineering in Iran (ICME 2022)

Abstract

This conference paper presents an innovative approach to recognizing patterns in force distributions within unbalanced rigid rotor bearing systems. The methodology combines signal processing techniques with machine learning to identify characteristic signatures of various imbalance conditions. The proposed approach enables more accurate diagnosis and proactive maintenance strategies for industrial rotating machinery.
