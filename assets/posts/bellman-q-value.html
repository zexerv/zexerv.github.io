The Bellman Optimal Q-Value Function

In reinforcement learning, the Bellman optimality equation forms the backbone of many learning algorithms. This post explores the Q-value function and its role in determining optimal policies.

\[ Q^*(s, a) = \mathbb{E} \left[ r + \gamma \max_{a'} Q^*(s', a') \mid s, a \right] \]

Equation Components

  • \( s \) Current state
  • \( a \) Chosen action
  • \( r \) Immediate reward
  • \( \gamma \) Discount factor
  • \( s' \) Subsequent state

Key Takeaways

  • Q-values represent expected future rewards
  • The Bellman equation enables dynamic programming solutions
  • Optimal policies emerge from value iteration
