About Me

A journey of exploration and discovery in robotics and beyond

My Journey

My story began in childhood, when family gatherings sparked a fascination with inventors—individuals who don't just accept the world as it is, but who strive to reshape it. This early drive crystallized during high school, where I stood firm in choosing mathematics over biology, despite external pressures. Math felt like more than just numbers; it was a language with which I could talk to the universe—a language that explained the world's intricacies.

In my undergraduate studies at KNTU, I embraced Mechanical Engineering, where my curiosity grew into a disciplined pursuit of knowledge. Early in my journey, I worked on an autonomous emergency braking system, applying object detection and depth estimation algorithms. This experience allowed me to witness firsthand how machines could learn to solve real-world problems, sparking my passion for intelligent systems and the power of automation.

"We can be the driver of the new things around us as opposed to being a passive consumer... going forward it's not only trying to understand the stuff that has grown naturally, it's gonna be understanding the stuff that we create."

— Brian Greene

The rise of reinforcement learning in robotics felt like a revolution to me. After completing my bachelor's thesis, I spent a portion of my semester diving into RL, drawn by its potential to redefine how machines learn and make decisions. This exploration inspired the question that continues to guide me: What is the best control policy for robots? Is there a universal framework that can govern all robotic systems, akin to physicists' search for the elusive M-theory?

To align my education with my vision, I pursued a Master's in Automation and Control Engineering at Politecnico di Milano. During my studies, I engaged in several research projects that sharpened both my technical and collaborative skills. At AIRLAB, I contributed to multi-agent drone autonomy, focusing on trajectory planning and control. Building on this foundation, I am currently working on the ARTO project, which focuses on teaching robots to learn tasks from human demonstrations.

Philosophy & Perspective

Over time, I've come to see science as humanity's most profound gift. Unlike other animals, we inherit not only our genes but also our knowledge—the accumulated science of generations before us. I borrow this inheritance from those who came before, expand on it with my own efforts, and hope to pass it on to those who come after me. This continuity of thought inspires me daily.

The pursuit of knowledge through exploration and experimentation

The COVID-19 pandemic was a turning point in my life. Amid the solitude and uncertainty, I found time to think deeply about the structure of scientific reasoning. I realized that true understanding requires stepping beyond the comfortable bounds of conformity, questioning the norms we live by, and embracing the unknown. During this period, I embraced science—not as a perfect entity, but as a guiding principle that acknowledges its own limitations.

Exploration has since become my guiding philosophy. In my academic journey, I've seen how this principle mirrors itself in the challenges of reinforcement learning—where "exploration" remains a crucial and unsolved problem. Beyond its technical meaning, exploration is a way of life: stepping into uncharted territories, testing boundaries, and learning from failures.

This mindset led me to cross continents, meet diverse people, and immerse myself in unfamiliar fields. Each step has strengthened my belief in the transformative power of curiosity and discovery. I believe that great innovation arises from the convergence of diverse ideas and perspectives—a concept reflected in one of the core challenges of reinforcement learning itself.

Research Interests

My research passion lies at the intersection of reinforcement learning and control systems. I want to explore how we can design intelligent robots that adapt and perform reliably in complex environments. This pursuit is not just about solving technical problems—it is about pushing the boundaries of human knowledge.

Human-Robot Interaction

Developing intuitive interfaces for non-technical users to program complex robot behaviors through demonstration and natural interaction.

Reinforcement Learning

Exploring the fundamental challenge of balancing exploration and exploitation in RL algorithms, with applications in robotic control and decision-making.

Control Theory

Bridging classical control approaches with modern learning-based methods to develop robust, adaptive controllers for complex mechanical systems.

System Integration

Creating unified frameworks that integrate perception, planning, and control for autonomous systems operating in dynamic environments.

Beyond Research

When I'm not working on robotics problems, I enjoy exploring the philosophical underpinnings of scientific thought. I'm particularly interested in how our understanding of intelligence can inform the development of artificial systems, and vice versa.

I also believe strongly in the importance of science communication and education. Through this website, my publications, and interactions with colleagues, I hope to contribute to broader conversations about the future of technology and its role in society.

Today, as I look at the road ahead, I feel a deep sense of purpose. My passion for science and exploration is not just a pursuit of knowledge; it is a commitment to contributing to humanity's collective understanding. I see myself as part of a continuum—an unbroken chain of inquiry that defines us as human.
