The Journey Begins

Welcome to my corner of the digital world! I'm Mo Saeidi, a robotics and control engineer passionate about the intersection of mathematics, machine learning, and mechanical systems. After years of absorbing knowledge and contributing to research papers, I've decided it's time to give back to the community that has supported my growth.

Why This Blog Exists

This blog serves as both a personal journal and a professional platform. There's something powerful about articulating complex concepts in your own words—it transforms passive understanding into active knowledge. As Richard Feynman famously said, "If you want to master something, teach it."

Here, I'll be sharing:

  • Technical insights from my work in robotics and control systems
  • Research explanations that break down complex academic papers into digestible pieces
  • Project walkthroughs documenting my engineering adventures
  • Learning reflections on my educational journey from mechanical engineering to robotics and AI

My Path So Far

My journey began with a deep fascination for how things move. As a mechanical engineering student, I was captivated by dynamics, motion planning, and the beautiful mathematics that govern physical systems. This led me to explore vibration analysis, control theory, and eventually, the exciting world of robotics.

Working in research labs across different countries has shaped my perspective on engineering challenges. From designing fault diagnosis systems in Tehran to developing drone navigation algorithms in Milan, each experience has added new dimensions to my understanding.

What particularly excites me now is the convergence of traditional control theory with modern machine learning approaches. Reinforcement learning, in particular, represents a fascinating bridge between these worlds—one that I'm actively exploring in my current research.

What's Next

In the coming weeks, I'll be sharing posts on:

  1. The mathematics behind reinforcement learning algorithms
  2. Practical applications of control theory in robotics
  3. My experiences with ROS and motion planning
  4. Insights from my current research projects

I don't claim to have all the answers—far from it. Consider this blog a documentation of my learning process, with all its messy explorations, unexpected discoveries, and occasional breakthroughs.

My hope is that by sharing these thoughts publicly, I can connect with fellow enthusiasts, researchers, and curious minds. Whether you're a seasoned engineer or simply interested in how robots learn to navigate our world, I hope you'll find something valuable here.

So here's to the beginning of a journey—one of continuous learning, thoughtful exploration, and shared discoveries. I'm excited to see where this path leads.

Feel free to reach out with questions, suggestions, or simply to connect over shared interests. The most rewarding journeys are rarely taken alone.

Until next time,
Mo
